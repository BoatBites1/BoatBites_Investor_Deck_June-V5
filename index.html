The Problem

Ecommerce stops at the shoreline

  • Boaters forget things, run out of things, crave things, and need things while already on the water.
  • On land, consumers expect instant access to food, drinks, groceries, and essentials.
  • On the water, the same convenience does not exist.
  • Current alternatives are inefficient: return to dock, call manually, overpack, or go without.

BoatBites is the only ecommerce + logistics platform built for life on the water.

Why This Is Hard

On-water commerce is not a DoorDash clone

  • Land-based delivery platforms stop at the shoreline.
  • Insurance, regulation, routing, and safety require water purpose-built systems.
  • Weather, safety, insurance, and compliance are built into the operating model.
  • These hard operating problems are why the category is still open.
  • Marine delivery requires accurate boat location, marina access, captain coordination, and docking protocols.
The insight

These difficulties are the reasons BoatBites can build a defensible category if it wins the access points and operating density before anyone knows how big we are.

Beachhead Strategy

Start where boating density, spending, and marina access create the fastest path to scale

  • Initial market focus: Florida.
  • Why Florida: high boating density, year-round season, marina concentration, high-income boaters, charter activity, and sandbar/event demand.
  • Expansion model: secure access, activate local merchants, recruit captains, and scale demand through marinas, social, charters, and events.
